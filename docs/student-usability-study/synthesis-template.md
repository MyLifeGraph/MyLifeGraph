## Findings ordered by severity

Use the observed denominator and evidence references. One accessibility,
privacy, data-loss, or false-authority problem may matter without repetition.

- Critical: blocks a core task, risks data/privacy, or creates a consequential
  false belief about an automatic mutation.
- Major: requires product help, repeated backtracking, or a repeated wrong
  interpretation that materially harms the journey.
- Minor: slows or distracts without changing the outcome.
- Observation: preference or isolated comment without enough behavioral
  evidence for a product defect.

## Capability-truth check

Summarize separately how many observed participants correctly understood:

- staged preview versus confirmed mutation;
- deterministic rules/example content versus actual Coach provider use;
- foreground-only Inbox delivery versus push/background delivery;
- read-only calendar import versus provider write-back;
- completed linked Focus versus manually entered prior preparation.

Use `x/n observed`, not a percentage that implies population precision.

## Completion checklist

- [ ] Five anonymized participant summaries exist.

- [ ] Part B gaps are marked `not observed` with honest denominators.
- [ ] Observations, quotes, and interpretations are separated.
- [ ] Findings cite participant codes and evidence references.
- [ ] Unique accessibility/safety issues were not discarded for low frequency.
- [ ] Product changes and justified non-changes are listed.
- [ ] Retests are distinguished from automated tests.
- [ ] No names, credentials, raw private data, or unsupported product claims
      appear in the repository artifact.
